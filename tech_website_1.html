What Exactly Are Mirrorless Cameras?

So what exactly are mirrorless cameras? Like commonly used DSLRs, mirrorless cameras are interchangeable lens cameras, meaning that the lens can be changed, and aren’t fixed on the body. The real difference between the DSLR and MILC (Mirrorless Cameras) is in the name itself. MILC don’t have a mirror that reflects the light to the viewfinder like traditional cameras; instead, they have an imaging sensor that always captures the light/image. Allowing the image to be constantly displayed on the electronic viewfinder or digital display. Due to the lack of mirrors, MILC are compact and small, since the space reserved for the mirrors are longer needed. However, because of this, MILC had some issues in the past.
